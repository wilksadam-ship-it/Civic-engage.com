About the Program

A substantive, evidence-based curriculum

Our course was developed by criminal justice educators and covers the real social, economic, and personal costs of retail theft — designed to meet court-ordered educational requirements.

4-Hour Curriculum

Standard Program Modules

Four modules covering the foundational topics required for court-ordered theft awareness education.

Module 1
The True Cost of Retail Theft

How theft affects prices, jobs, small businesses, and entire communities — beyond just the retailer.

Module 2
Legal Consequences & the Justice System

Criminal records, sentencing guidelines, civil recovery, and the long-term impact on employment and housing.

Module 3
Understanding the Decision to Steal

Psychological, social, and situational factors that contribute to theft — and how to recognize them.

Module 4
Moving Forward: Positive Change

Practical strategies for making better decisions, building accountability, and accessing support resources.

8-Hour Curriculum

Extended Program — Additional Modules

Includes all four modules above, plus four additional in-depth modules with case studies and deeper analysis.

Module 5
Retail Theft & Organized Crime

How individual theft connects to larger criminal networks, and the public safety implications.

Module 6
Victims of Theft: Human Impact

Case studies exploring how theft affects store employees, business owners, and surrounding neighborhoods.

Module 7
Economic & Societal Ripple Effects

Pricing, tax revenue, neighborhood investment, and how retail closures affect under-resourced communities.

Module 8
Restorative Justice & Accountability

An introduction to restorative justice principles and how accountability can be a path to genuine rehabilitation.
